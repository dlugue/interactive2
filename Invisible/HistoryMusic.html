THE HISTORY OF
THE UNITED STATES
HEARD THROUGH MUSIC
FROM THE PAST 50 YEARS

This is an analysis of how the everchanging atmosphere of the United States has shaped music thorughout the years. Music is not seen it is heard, but the content of music can create a visual representation of the times. What lyrically moved the national audience and what connected with the people of those times? What were the prevailing trends and what does that say about America?

Using the Billboard Top 100 list from each year starting at 1966, I’ve collected every number one through number five song (255 songs in total). Then after tagged each song with a theme, genre, and background.

Here are the results:

#1   SONG   PLAYLIST

A quick look at only the top song of each year

If we take only the most popular songs from each year what happens? To identify potential patterns and relationships, I put together this playlist where each song will play unless skipped, providing a concise overview. We can then note overlapping themes in genres and trends across the years. Revealing the ever evolving content that captured many.

115   LOVE   SONGS

Overwhelmingly deep feelings of affection

Unsurpisingly, or not depending on your outlook on life, love songs were the most common theme. Love is something anyone can relate to no matter the political or social climate. Sort of cheesy but then again maybe it’s the only constant we have to hold on to. Whether it's romantic or familial, love seems to be what people always come back to. "Some people want to fill the world with silly love songs, And what’s wrong with that?" - Paul McCartney

We've covered a ton of ground, 255 songs and 50 years worth of ground to be exact. And we've seen how musical taste between generations tend to shift. Looking back, common themes are found in all genres. Creating a history through music that is quite atmospheric as it reveals the general mood of the masses.

Music is often created to appeal, to connect with people and be relatable. What becomes popular should in theory reflect that. But what do you think? What music speaks to you and will your music preference help shape what future genrations look back on?

Only time will tell.
